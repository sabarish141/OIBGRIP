DR.APJ ABDUL KALAM

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was the 11th President of India, serving from 2002 to 2007. Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India on October 15, 1931, and passed away on July 27, 2015 in Shillong, Meghalaya, India.

A aerospace engineer and known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on the development of India's civilian space program and military missiles. Played a key role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, which established India as a nuclear power. After serving as President, Dr. Kalam became a professor and an author, writing several books on his experience and vision for India's future. A motivational speaker and inspired millions of people, especially young students, through his speeches and teachings. Received numerous honors and awards for his contributions, including the Padma Bhushan in 1981, the Padma Vibhushan in 1990, and the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, in 1997. Remembered as a visionary leader and a true patriot who dedicated his life to serving his country and inspiring future generations.
